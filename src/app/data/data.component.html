LIDAR Data

"Unprocessed LiDAR frame from a roadside Velodyne VLP-16 sensor, showing how resulting point-cloud data fix and can track objects precisely in three dimensions."

Displays a 3D visualization of pedestrian LiDAR data points, captured for onboard Connected Autonomous Vehicle (CAV) sensing. This graphic illustrates two clusters of points, showing 484 points in one cluster and 512 points in another, both at a 3-meter detection range. The X, Y, and Z axes denote the spatial dimensions in meters, highlighting the density and distribution of LiDAR data points used to analyze pedestrian movement and presence in a three-dimensional space. This visualization is critical for understanding how LiDAR technology captures and processes pedestrian data in real-world scenarios, aiding in the development of sophisticated algorithms for object identification and tracking.

The image is an unprocessed LiDAR frame captured by a roadside Velodyne VLP-16 sensor. The system effectively filters out over 97% of background points, even in high-traffic areas, allowing for accurate detection and identification of various road users, including vehicles, pedestrians, and bicycles.
